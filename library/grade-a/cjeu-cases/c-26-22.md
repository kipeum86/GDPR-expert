## C-26/22 — SCHUFA I (Scoring)

**Parties:** SCHUFA Holding AG (Scoring)

**Judgment Date:** 2023-12-07

**ECLI:** ECLI:EU:C:2023:988

**Significance:** landmark

### Key Holding

The CJEU held that the establishment of a probability score by a credit reference agency constitutes 'automated individual decision-making' under Art. 22 GDPR where that score is transmitted to a third party that relies heavily on it for its own decision. This broadened the scope of Art. 22 significantly, with major implications for AI-based and algorithmic decision-making across all sectors.

### Related GDPR Articles

Art. 22, Art. 6(1)(f)
