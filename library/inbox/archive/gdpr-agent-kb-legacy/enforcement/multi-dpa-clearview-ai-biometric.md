# Multiple DPAs v Clearview AI - Biometric Data Enforcement

## Case Information
- **Authorities:** Italian Garante (March 2022), French CNIL (October 2022), Greek HDPA (July 2022), UK ICO (May 2022)
- **Target:** Clearview AI Inc.
- **Fines:** EUR 20M (Italy), EUR 20M (France), EUR 20M (Greece), GBP 7.5M (UK, later overturned)
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 6, 9, 14 GDPR

## Facts

Clearview AI scraped over 20 billion facial images from the internet (social media, news sites, etc.) without consent and created a facial recognition database marketed to law enforcement agencies worldwide. The company had no EU establishment.

## Violations Found (Common Across DPAs)

1. **No legal basis (Art. 6):** No valid legal basis for mass scraping of facial images
2. **Special categories (Art. 9):** Biometric data processed without any Art. 9(2) exception
3. **Right to information (Art. 14):** No information provided to data subjects whose images were scraped
4. **Purpose limitation:** Images collected for one purpose (social media sharing) repurposed for surveillance

## Significance

- First coordinated multi-DPA enforcement against facial recognition technology
- Establishes that web scraping of biometric data violates GDPR regardless of "public availability"
- Extraterritorial enforcement against US company with no EU establishment
- "Publicly available" does not equal "manifestly made public" under Art. 9(2)(e)
