# CJEU C-61/19: Orange Romania (Freely Given Consent)

## Case Information
- **Court:** Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Case Number:** C-61/19
- **Decision Date:** November 11, 2020

## Facts

Orange Romania, a mobile telecommunications provider, obtained and stored photocopies of customers' identity documents without valid consent. The Romanian DPA (ANSPDCP) imposed administrative penalties. Orange's consent mechanism involved pre-ticked boxes on some contracts and requirements for customers to handwrite refusals on a separate form to decline consent.

## Key Holdings

Consent is **invalid** when:
1. The checkbox referring to ID storage consent was pre-ticked by the controller
2. Contract terms mislead subjects about whether refusal prevents contract conclusion
3. Freedom to object is "unduly affected" by requiring completion of an additional form

The judgment emphasized that valid consent demands active behavior from data subjects, not passive acceptance of pre-selected options.

## Significance

Establishes strict standards for consent validity in contractual relationships. Aligns with Planet49 precedent regarding consent requirements. The "freely given" requirement means controllers cannot make consent a practical necessity through procedural obstacles.
