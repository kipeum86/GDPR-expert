## CJEU — SCHUFA Credit Scoring (Art. 22 Ruling)

**DPA:** CJEU (Preliminary ruling; referred by German courts)

**Target Entity:** SCHUFA Holding AG

**Fine Amount:** No fine (CJEU preliminary ruling)

**Decision Date:** 2023-12-07

**Significance:** landmark

### Summary

The CJEU ruled (Case C-634/21) that creating a credit score constitutes 'automated individual decision-making' under Art. 22 GDPR when third-party lenders 'draw strongly' on these scores for contract decisions. This landmark ruling means credit scoring agencies must comply with Art. 22's restrictions including the right to obtain human intervention, to express one's point of view, and to contest the decision. The case was remanded to German courts for further proceedings.

### Violated Articles

Art. 22(1)
