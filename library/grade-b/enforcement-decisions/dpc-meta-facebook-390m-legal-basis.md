# DPC Ireland v Meta (Facebook) - EUR 390 Million Fine

## Case Information
- **Authority:** Data Protection Commission (DPC), Ireland
- **Decision Date:** January 4, 2023
- **Target:** Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd (Facebook)
- **Fine:** EUR 390,000,000 (EUR 210M for Facebook + EUR 180M for Instagram)
- **Violated Articles:** Art. 6(1)(b), Art. 5(1)(a) GDPR

## Facts

The DPC investigated Meta's reliance on "contractual necessity" (Art. 6(1)(b)) as the legal basis for processing personal data for personalized/behavioral advertising on Facebook and Instagram. Meta included consent to personalized advertising in its Terms of Service, arguing this made such processing necessary for contract performance.

## Violations Found

1. **Art. 6(1)(b):** Personalized advertising is not "objectively necessary" for performing the core Facebook/Instagram service contract. Users can use the service without personalized ads.
2. **Art. 5(1)(a) - Transparency:** Meta failed to clearly inform users that their personal data would be processed for behavioral advertising under the contractual necessity legal basis.

## EDPB Binding Decision

The EDPB adopted binding decisions (3/2022 for Facebook, 4/2022 for Instagram) requiring the DPC to find Art. 6(1)(b) violations and increase fines.

## Significance

- Definitively establishes that behavioral advertising cannot rely on "contractual necessity"
- Major platforms must obtain separate, explicit consent for advertising data processing
- EDPB's binding decision mechanism proved decisive in overriding lead DPA's initial assessment
